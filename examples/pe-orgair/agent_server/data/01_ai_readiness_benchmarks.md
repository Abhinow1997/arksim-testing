# AI Readiness in Enterprise Technology Companies

## Data Infrastructure Maturity

Modern enterprise tech companies are increasingly investing in unified data platforms. A mature data infrastructure typically includes:

- **Real-time data pipelines**: Apache Kafka or AWS Kinesis for streaming ingestion
- **Data lakehouse architecture**: Delta Lake or Apache Iceberg unifying batch and streaming
- **Feature stores**: Centralized repositories (Feast, Tecton) for ML feature reuse
- **Data governance**: Automated lineage tracking, PII detection, and access controls
- **Data quality**: Great Expectations or Monte Carlo for automated validation

Companies at the Pioneering tier maintain sub-second data freshness for 90%+ of their ML models and have achieved full data mesh implementations across business units.

## AI Talent Landscape

Leading technology companies employ 500–2,000+ AI/ML practitioners. Key roles include:
- Applied Scientists and Research Scientists (PhD-level ML research)
- ML Engineers (production model deployment and scaling)
- Data Scientists (analytics, experimentation, model development)
- MLOps Engineers (infrastructure, monitoring, CI/CD for models)
- AI Product Managers (translating business needs into AI roadmaps)

Companies like Salesforce, Microsoft, and Google have dedicated AI Research labs and publish extensively in NLP, computer vision, and reinforcement learning. Annual AI R&D spend often exceeds $1B for top-tier companies.

## Technology Stack Benchmarks

Enterprise AI leaders typically use:
- **Cloud**: Multi-cloud strategy (AWS + Azure or GCP) for resilience
- **MLOps**: MLflow, Kubeflow, or Vertex AI for experiment tracking and deployment
- **Vector databases**: Pinecone, Weaviate, or pgvector for RAG applications
- **Model serving**: TorchServe, Triton Inference Server for low-latency inference
- **Observability**: Arize, WhyLabs, or Fiddler for model monitoring and drift detection

## SEC Filing Disclosure Patterns

When companies disclose AI capabilities in 10-K filings, common signals include:
- "AI-powered" product mentions in business description sections
- R&D expense line items attributed to "machine learning research"
- Risk factors mentioning "competition from AI-native companies"
- Management discussion of "AI integration" across product lines
- Mentions of specific AI partnerships (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind)
