# AI Governance and Ethics Frameworks

## Responsible AI Program Components

A mature AI Governance program for a PE-backed company should include:

### Policy Layer
- **Acceptable Use Policy**: Defines permitted and prohibited AI applications
- **Bias and Fairness Standards**: Quantitative thresholds for demographic parity
- **Model Risk Management (MRM)**: Tiered review process for high-stakes models
- **AI Incident Response Plan**: Documented procedures for model failures

### Technical Controls
- **Model Cards**: Standardized documentation for every deployed model
- **Bias auditing**: Automated checks using Fairlearn or IBM AI Fairness 360
- **Explainability**: SHAP/LIME integration for model interpretability
- **Data lineage**: End-to-end tracking from raw data to model predictions

### Organizational Controls
- **AI Ethics Board**: Cross-functional committee reviewing high-risk AI deployments
- **Red teaming**: Adversarial testing before major AI feature launches
- **Third-party audits**: Annual external AI risk assessments

## Regulatory Environment

Companies operating in regulated sectors face additional AI governance requirements:
- **EU AI Act**: High-risk AI systems require conformity assessments (effective 2026)
- **NIST AI RMF**: Voluntary framework increasingly adopted as industry standard
- **FTC Guidelines**: Unfair or deceptive AI practices enforcement
- **SEC Cybersecurity Rules**: AI system incidents may trigger 8-K disclosure obligations

## Governance Maturity Levels

| Level | Characteristics |
|-------|----------------|
| Nascent | No formal AI policy; ad-hoc model approvals |
| Developing | Basic acceptable use policy; some documentation |
| Advancing | Formal MRM process; ethics committee exists |
| Leading | Full bias auditing; third-party assessments; EU AI Act ready |
| Pioneering | Published ethics reports; open-source governance tools contributed |
