# Culture and Change Readiness for AI Adoption

## AI Culture Assessment Framework

Organizational culture is often the biggest barrier to AI value creation.
PE firms should assess the following dimensions:

### Employee AI Literacy
- **Baseline**: Do individual contributors understand what AI can/cannot do?
- **Measurement**: AI literacy assessment scores; training completion rates
- **Best practice**: Mandatory AI fluency training for all employees (not just technical)
- **Target**: 80%+ of workforce completes AI awareness training within 12 months

### Experimentation Culture
- **Signals**: Number of internal AI hackathons per year
- **Signals**: Dedicated innovation time (e.g., 20% time for AI experiments)
- **Signals**: Fail-fast culture with documented learnings from unsuccessful AI projects
- **Red flag**: Fear of automation leading to shadow IT or AI avoidance

### Cross-Functional Collaboration
- **Center of Excellence (CoE)**: Dedicated AI CoE with embedded data scientists
- **Guilds / communities of practice**: Informal AI learning communities
- **Executive sponsorship**: C-suite champion for AI transformation
- **Incentive alignment**: Performance reviews include AI adoption metrics

### Change Management Programs
- **Reskilling programs**: Upskilling non-technical roles for AI-augmented workflows
- **Communication**: Regular AI town halls and transparency about automation impacts
- **Governance**: Clear process for employees to flag AI concerns
- **Success stories**: Internal case study sharing of AI wins

## Culture Readiness Red Flags for PE

- High attrition in data science teams (>20% annually)
- Lack of executive sponsorship at VP level or above
- AI projects consistently failing to move from pilot to production
- Business units operating independently without data sharing
- Fear-based resistance: Employees hiding AI usage from management
- No documented AI wins communicated internally in the past 12 months
